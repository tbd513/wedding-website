The Right Time and Place

We met on Mt.Hood in the spring of 2013. Two career focused, active, big personality Portlander’s, that at the time found no connection beyond another person to wave at while on the mountain. Later that fall during a chance meet up for coffee and lunch, conversations sparked interests in one another, revealing striking similarities (we both have brothers for example). As winter approached we transitioned from coffee drinking buddies to snowboarding mates, soon realizing there was more to our story. Little did we know that our initial lunchbreak meetups and flirtations on the mountain would result in an unbreakable friendship and the love for each other we have today.

By News Years 2016, our three year anniversary together, any doubt of our compatibility and love for one another was left far behind as we set out for another adventure together. Nikki didn’t know at the time but I was waiting for a day together in the mountain so I could propose.

Engagement

That day came one month later while exploring British Columbia during a February ski vacation. On our final day in the mountains we found ourselves in an isolated grove of trees without anyone around. Stumbling my words, I slowly proposed to Nikki and she quickly accepted, allowing me to slip the giant fake rock onto her finger.

We were both wrapped up in the moment and almost left the woods without a visual reminder of the occasion. Wiping tears from my cheek, trying to regain composure, it was Nikki who smartly suggested we snap a photo together before we emerge from the woods as an engaged couple.

Venue Decision

Having both lived in Portland, Oregon for the past eight years and having met each other on Mt.Hood, it is only fitting that we have our family and friends unite here. Planning a wedding in less than a year has lead us to a location less traveled and we couldn’t be more happy with this decision. The Husum Highlands Bed and Breakfast is a beautiful mountain venue that’s a bit off the beaten wedding trail.
